PTD TO MCQ MAKING

PTD (Paragraph-Type Discourse) is converted into MCQs by framing a clear question and providing options, with one correct answer.
STEPS TO CONVERT PTD TO MCQ
1

READ THE PTD CAREFULLY

Understand the main idea and key points of the passage.

2

IDENTIFY THE KEY CONCEPT

Pick the most important idea to frame the question.

3

FRAME A CLEAR QUESTION

The question should be specific, concise and based on the PTD.

4

PREPARE OPTIONS

Write four options - one correct and three plausible distractors.

5

CHECK AND FINALIZE

Ensure the question is accurate, options are relevant, and only one is correct.

Tips: Options should be homogeneous, plausible, and of similar length.

EXAMPLE
PTD:

Education plays a vital role in the development of an individual and the progress of a society. It not only imparts knowledge but also develops values, skills and attitudes. It enables people to make informed decisions, contribute to the economy and promote social harmony. Therefore, education is essential for personal growth and national development.

MCQ:

What is the main purpose of education as stated in the passage?

A. To impart knowledge only
B. To develop values, skills and promote growth
C. To contribute to the economy only
D. To promote social harmony only
Correct Answer: B

REMEMBER: A good MCQ tests understanding, not memory!
